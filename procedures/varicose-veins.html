Varicose Veins Treatment

Modern, walk-in walk-out treatment for varicose veins using laser, radiofrequency, and sclerotherapy no hospital admission, no general anaesthesia, no long scars.

Vascular Procedure

What are Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are enlarged, twisted veins that appear just beneath the skin surface, most commonly in the legs. They develop when the one-way valves inside the leg veins become weakened or damaged, allowing blood to pool and the vein walls to stretch and bulge. While often considered a cosmetic concern, varicose veins can cause significant symptoms and, if left untreated, can progress to chronic venous insufficiency, skin changes, and venous ulcers.

Common symptoms include aching, heaviness, or throbbing in the legs especially after prolonged standing as well as swelling around the ankles, itching over the veins, skin discolouration, and in advanced cases, leg ulcers. Risk factors include prolonged standing or sitting, pregnancy, family history, obesity, and advancing age.

When Should You Seek Treatment?

  • Significant leg aching, heaviness, or fatigue that affects daily activities
  • Swelling of the ankle or lower leg that worsens through the day
  • Skin changes over the inner ankle (lipodermatosclerosis, pigmentation)
  • Healed or active venous leg ulcers
  • Superficial thrombophlebitis (painful clot in a varicose vein)
  • Cosmetic concern causing significant distress

What to Expect on Procedure Day?

  • A pre-procedure duplex ultrasound maps the vein anatomy and identifies incompetent valves
  • Local anaesthetic tumescent solution is infiltrated around the target vein under ultrasound guidance
  • The laser fibre or RFA catheter is advanced along the vein and energy delivered as it is withdrawn
  • The entire procedure takes 45–75 minutes per leg
  • You walk out of the clinic within 30 minutes of the procedure
  • Compression stockings are worn for 1–2 weeks; normal activities resume the same day

Benefits of Minimally Invasive Treatment

Walk-In Walk-Out

No hospital admission, no general anaesthesia. You walk out of the procedure room and drive home.

No Surgical Scars

Only tiny needle access points no long surgical incisions running down your leg.

Immediate Relief

Most patients notice reduction in heaviness and aching within days. Cosmetic improvement follows over weeks.

High Durability

EVLA and RFA have 5-year success rates >95%, far superior to conventional surgery.

Frequently Asked Questions

A duplex ultrasound scan of your leg veins is the essential first step. This maps the anatomy, identifies the source of reflux, and determines which veins need treatment. Dr. Shetty reviews this with you and recommends the most appropriate approach EVLA, RFA, sclerotherapy, or a combination based on your vein anatomy and symptoms.
The most uncomfortable part is the injection of local anaesthetic tumescent solution around the vein, which feels like multiple small pinpricks. Once numb, the laser or RFA energy delivery is typically painless. Most patients describe the overall experience as very tolerable far more comfortable than they expected.
Light work (desk job) can typically be resumed the same or next day. Walking is actively encouraged from day one. Heavy exercise (gym, running) should be avoided for 2 weeks. You should wear compression stockings throughout this period.
EVLA and RFA have excellent durability with published 5-year closure rates of 93–96% for the treated vein. New varicose veins can appear over time in other branches, as the underlying tendency toward venous valve failure may continue. Regular follow-up and weight management help minimise recurrence risk.
Coverage depends on your insurance policy and the clinical indication. Most insurers cover treatment when there are significant symptoms (pain, swelling, skin changes, ulcers) documented by a physician. Purely cosmetic procedures are generally not covered. Our team can help you with the documentation needed for pre-authorisation.
